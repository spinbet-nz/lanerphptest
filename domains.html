Domains & DNS, explained from scratch

Every website starts with a domain name. Here's what a domain really is, how registrars such as Squarespace Domains work, and how DNS quietly connects your name to the right server.

The basics

What is a domain name?

A domain name is the human-friendly address people type to reach your website — like example.com. Behind the scenes, computers talk to each other using numeric IP addresses; the domain name is a memorable label that maps to one of those addresses.

Domains are organised into extensions called TLDs (top-level domains): .com .org .io .dev and hundreds more. You don't "buy" a domain forever — you register the right to use it, usually one year at a time, and renew it to keep it.

How it works

From registration to a live website

Choose & register a name

Search a registrar for an available name and register it. A registrar is an ICANN-accredited company authorised to sell domain registrations — for example, Squarespace Domains.

Point DNS records

DNS (the Domain Name System) is the web's address book. You add records — like an A record or CNAME — that tell browsers which server hosts your site.

Connect your hosting

Link the domain to your website builder or hosting provider. Once DNS propagates (usually minutes to a few hours), visitors reach your site by name.

Add SSL & renew

Enable HTTPS with an SSL certificate for a secure padlock, then keep the domain renewed so you never lose it.

FAQ

Common domain questions

Do I own my domain forever?expand_more
No — you register the exclusive right to use it for a set period (often one year) and renew to keep it. If you let it expire, someone else can register it.
What's the difference between a registrar and a host?expand_more
A registrar manages your domain name; a host stores your website's files and serves them to visitors. They can be the same company or two different ones.
How long does DNS take to update?expand_more
Changes usually take effect within minutes, but full global propagation can take up to 24–48 hours depending on cached records.
Should I pay for WHOIS privacy?expand_more
Privacy protection hides your personal contact details from public WHOIS lookups. Many registrars, including Squarespace Domains, now include it free.
